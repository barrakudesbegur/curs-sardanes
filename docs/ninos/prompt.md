# Generate images of ninos with AI

## Workflow

This image has many "ninos" and a empty space. Pass it to the AI so it adds a new nino to the empty slot. Modt of the times it generates a incorrect style or ugly asset, but others it is very isefull to figure out the pose of the nino. When you get a good one, then you can isolate it and vectorize it as a path of 28px thickness and 24px thickness for the details (such as eyes). I have a figma with all of them.

## Propt to generate images of ninos with AI

### Sumary

Blob-like humanoid figures drawn with a single continuous thick black outline on white. Other outlines are slightly thiner. Bodies are rounded, amorphous shapes, no sharp angles or straight lines. Eyes are two side-by-side ovals. No mouth. Limbs are short, stubby, noodle-like extensions of the body blob, no joints. Figures are expressive through poses (dancing, having fun...) and accessories (hats, props, particles...). Overall feel: playful, minimal, doodle-like, Keith Haring meets gummy bear.

### Dos

- Every figure must be doing something—acting out a situation, embodying a stereotype, or being funny; they should feel alive, mid-action, and full of personality
- Use a single continuous thick black stroke for the entire figure
- Make bodies blobby, organic, rounded—like a gingerbread man melted slightly
- Draw eyes as two connected or adjacent ovals, always the same style
- Keep limbs as short rounded nubs extending from the blob body
- Express action/emotion purely through body pose
- Accessories (hats, tools, wings) follow the same thick-outline blob style
- Leave the interior white/empty—no shading or fill
- Keep proportions chunky: big head-body blob, small limbs
- Use a thick black stroke for the body contour and a slightly thinner stroke for eyes, accessories, props, and detail marks

### Don’ts

- Use thin lines, varying line weights, or sketchy strokes
- Add mouths, noses, ears, or facial detail beyond the oval eyes
- Draw fingers, toes, hands, or feet
- Use straight lines, sharp corners, or geometric shapes for the body
- Add shading, crosshatching, gradients, or color fills
- Make stick-figure proportions—figures must be thick and blobby
- Use realistic anatomy or defined joints (elbows, knees, wrists)
- Detach the eyes from the head—they sit embedded in the top of the blob
- Add internal detail lines within the body silhouette
- Draw static, boring, stiff, or symmetrically standing poses—every figure needs energy and movement
